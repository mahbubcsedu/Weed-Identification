Virginia Buttonweed ( Diodia virginiana)

Virginia buttonweed grows in trails along the aground. The leaves are elliptical to lanceolate, and can grow to be around 1 to 2 inches long to around 1 inch wide. If infected with a common virus, the normally green leaves can have a yellow mottling.

Common Ragweed ( Ambrosia artemisiifolia)

Milkweed stands 3 to 5 feet tall, and has large, oblong leaves. The leaves tend to be 4 to 12 inches long and 2 to 4 inches wide. Milkweed flowers grow in clusters of pink or purple, and droop slightly. Milkweed also has distinct gray-green teardrop shaped seedpods that are feathery and tufted.

Hophornbean Copperleaf ( Acalypha ostryaefolia)

Hophornbeam copperleaf plants can grow up to 40 inches tall. The leaves have red coloration near the base of the leaf. The leaves are egg or diamond shaped, with toothed edges. The flowers grow in long spikes.

†

Horseweed ( Conzya canadensis)

A mature horseweed plant can reach a height of six and a half feet, but the young plants are flat, round spreads of egg-shaped leaves shown above. The leaves on a mature plant can grow to be four inches long with large toothed edges. Horseweed flowers are white, slight pink or slight yellow, and grow at the top of the central stem.

†

Jimsonweed ( Datura stramonium)

Jimsonweed has large white and purple flowers that vary from 2 to 5 inches in size, and the fruit is egg-shaped and covered in stiff spines. Each fruit is 1 to 2 inches large. Jimsonweed plants have greenish purple stems. The leaves can grow up to 6 inches long and 3 to 8 inches wide and tend to be oval shaped with large triangular teeth.

†

Kochia ( Kochia scoparia)

Kochia leaves grow along the stem reaching lengths of 1 to 2 inches. They are linear and taper to a point. The flowers row in clusters and the ends of the stem and in between the leaf bases. The flowers are green in color and are inconspicuous. The stems of kochia plants are highly branched.

Pokeweed ( Phytoloacca americana)

Pokeweed is a large plant with a thick, smooth stem. Young plants have egg-shaped leaves that lengthen into maturity. The leaves end up being 3.5 to 12 inches long and 1 to 4 inches wide. Pokeweed‚Äôs flowers are small, with five white or pink petals. The pokeweed had distinctive berries that are dark-purple or black when mature and contain dark red juice. Immature berries are green or white.

Venice Mallow ( Hibiscus trionium)

Venice mallow leaves are divided into at least three distinct lobes, but can develop up to 7. The leaves grow to up to 3 inches wide and long. All lobes have large toothed edges. The stem of the Venice mallow grows erect, and the flowers are white or pale yellow with a purple center. The flowers have five petals that can have distinctive green veins. The fruit is green, round and hairy.

Canada Thistle ( Cirsium arvense)

Canada thistle has erect stems, and can grow up to 4 feet tall. Young Canada thistle leaves are covered in short hairs. As they mature, the leaves develop irregular lobes with triangular dentations and spiny edges. The upper surface of the leaves tends to be dark green, while the bottom is lighter. Canada thistle grows numerous flowers from .75 to 1.25 inches in diameter that are pink, purple and rarely white.
