Carolina Geranium ( Geranium carolinianum)

†

The Carolina Geranium grows upright, with erect stems that can grow up to 28 inches tall. The stems are usually pink or red in color, with dense hairs. The leaves grow from .75 to 2.5 inches wide and are rounded, with deep divides separating the leaf into 5 to 9 segments. Each segment has large rounded toothed edges.

Purple Cudweed ( Gnaphalium purpureum)

Purple cudweed grows stems erect from the base plant. They are grayish-white with a woolly appearance. Leaves on the base are 4 inches long and .75 inches wide. Leaves on the stem are progressively smaller the further towards the top they grow. The edges can be both smooth or with very large, curved teeth. The leaves are gray-green in color.

Fireweed ( Erechtites hieraciifolia)

†

Fireweed stems grow erect and are usually without hairs, but can occasionally be slightly haired. The leaves are elliptic to lanceolate in outline and grow to be around 2 to 8 inches long and .5 to 2.5 inches wide. The leaves come to a distinct point on the tip, and the edges are irregularly toothed. Fireweed flowers can grow at the ends of stems and are cream to pinkish in color. Prostrate Knotweed ( Polygonum aviculare)

Prostrate Knotweed grows low on the ground with many branches that range from 4 to 24 inches long. Stems are swollen at the growth nodes. The leaves are small, lanceolate in outline and grow to be about .5 to 1.25 inches long. Clusters of 1 to 5 flowers occur between the stems and the leaves. The flowers are small, white to pink-white and are inconspicuous.

†

Common Lespedeza ( Lespedeza striata)

Lespedeza grows on branching stems. The leaves consist of three oblong leaflets that from from .5 to .75 inches long and .33 to .5 inches wide, and are much narrower at the base than the tip.

†

Mulberry Weed ( Fatoua villosa)

†

Mullberry weed can grow to be around 3 to 4 feet tall. The leaves are egg-shaped with serrated toothed edges. Mulbeery weed flowers grow purplish green in 1-inch clusters without petals.

†

Parsley Piert ( Aphanes microcarpa)

Parsley piert grows on wide, branching stems that can reach 1 to 6 feet tall. The leaves are around .5 inches in diameter with a distinct lobed /toothed pattern. The flowers are green and inconspicuous.

Spotted Burclover ( Medicago arabica)

Spotted burclover grows on erect stems. The leaves are triangle-shaped, and develop a distinctive browning-purple spot in their center. Spotted burclover flowers grow in clusters of 2 to 5, and are bright yellow. The seedpods are green, round and spiked.

†

Common Milkweed ( Asclepias syriaca)

Milkweed stands 3 to 5 feet tall, and has large, oblong leaves. The leaves tend to be 4 to 12 inches long and 2 to 4 inches wide. Milkweed flowers grow in clusters of pink or purple, and droop slightly. Milkweed also has distinct gray-green teardrop shaped seedpods that are feathery and tufted.
