Large-Flowering Weed Training

Common Catsear (Hypochaeris radicata)

Common catsear has leaves that are densely hairy and can be toothed or irregularly lobed. Commons catsear does not have typical stems, but rather leafless flower stalks which can have 2 to 7 flowers each. The flowers are bright yellow, with the many petals emanating from the center. Catsear petals have distinctive toothing at the tips of the petals.

 

Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale)

Dandelions have erect stems that can grow from 2 to six inches in height. Dandelion leaves have a distinctive wave pattern, oblong shape, and range from 2 to 16 inches in length. The flowers are bright yellow in color, and are typically 1.25 to 2 inches in diameter. The flowers grow on the end on leafless stalks, only bearing one flower per stalk. Dandelions also have a distinctive “puffball” seedhead.

 

Facelis (Facelis ratusa)

Facelis is a low growing weed, with branching stems that can reach around 2 to 4 inches in height. Facelis produces a white “puff ball” seedhead.

 

Fireweed (Erechtites hieraciifolia)

 

Fireweed stems grow erect and are usually without hairs, but can occasionally be slightly haired. The leaves are elliptic to lanceolate in outline and grow to be around 2 to 8 inches long and .5 to 2.5 inches wide. The leaves come to a distinct point on the tip, and the edges are irregularly toothed. Fireweed flowers can grow at the ends of stems and are cream to pinkish in color. The seedpods are white “puffballs” with long tufted hairs.

 

Large Hop Clover (Trifolium campestre)

Large hop clover can grow to be around 1 foot tall, with many branches on its green to reddish green stems. The leaves grow in bunches of three oval-shaped leaflets, and have lightly toothed edges. The leaves also have distinctive veining. The yellow flower heads grow to be about .5 inches in diameter, and are spherical bunches of around 15 to 40 individual flowers.

 

Common Milkweed (Asclepias syriaca)

Milkweed stands 3 to 5 feet tall, and has large, oblong leaves. The leaves tend to be 4 to 12 inches long and 2 to 4 inches wide. Milkweed flowers grow in clusters of pink or purple, and droop slightly. Milkweed also has distinct gray-green teardrop shaped seedpods that are feathery and tufted.

 

Jimsonweed (Datura stramonium )

Jimsonweed has large white and purple flowers that vary from 2 to 5 inches in size, and the fruit is egg-shaped and covered in stiff spines. Each fruit is 1 to 2 inches large. Jimsonweed plants have greenish purple stems. The leaves can grow up to 6 inches long and 3 to 8 inches wide and tend to be oval shaped with large triangular teeth.

 

Venice Mallow (Hibiscus trionium)

Venice mallow leaves are divided into at least three distinct lobes, but can develop up to 7. The leaves grow to up to 3 inches wide and long. All lobes have large toothed edges. The stem of the Venice mallow grows erect, and the flowers are white or pale yellow with a purple center. The flowers have five petals that can have distinctive green veins. The fruit is green, round and hairy.

 

Oxeye Daisy (Chrysanthemum Leucanthemum)

 

Oxeye daisies grow erect without branches to about one foot in height. The plant contains a single flower at the end of the step that contains 20 to 30 white outer petals and many inner yellow flowers. The flowers range from 1.25 to 2 inches in diameter. The young plant starts out with small leaves with large rounded teeth. Mature plants have leaves that are lanceolate in outline and become progressively smaller up the stem.
