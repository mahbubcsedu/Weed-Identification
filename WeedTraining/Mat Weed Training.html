Mat Weed Training

Carpetweed ( Mollugo verticillata)

Carpetweed grows along the ground with branching stems that are green or greenish brown. The stems can grow from 2 to 12 inches long. The leaves are smooth and rounded tapering at their base, and they tend to grow in whorls of leaves at stem nodes. The small white flowers grow in clusters of 2 to 5, and they have 5 petals each.

 

Common Chickweed ( Stellaria media)

Chickweed leaves are wide and elliptical. They grow to be about .5 to 1 inches long. Chickweed flowers have five petals with such a deep split that the flowers appear to have ten petals. Chickweed tends to grow in dense mats.

 

Dichondra ( Dichondra repens)

 

Dichondra grows along the ground, with brown to green stems. Dichondra leaves are circular to kidney-shaped.

 

Hairy Bittercress ( Cardamine hirsuta)

Hairy bittercress grows on erect stems that branch in multiple directions from the plant base. The stems can grow as much as 12 inches tall. The leaves consist of clusters of 2 to 4 leaflets that grow along the central stem. Hairy bittercress flowers grow in clusters at the ends of flowering stems. The flowers are small, white and consist of 4 petals.

 

Spotted Spurge ( Chamaescye maculata)

Spotted spurge grows close to the ground from a central point. The stems are pink to red colored and densely haired. The leaves are egg-shaped and can grow to be just over .5 inches long. The leaves also have a brownish-purple spot in their center.

 

Purslane ( Portulaca oleracea)

Purslane has smooth purplish red to green stems that grow low along the ground with lots of branches. Its leaves are long and rounded at the ends but narrowed at the base, with smooth edges. The leaves grow from .25 to 1.25 inches long. Purslane flowers are small and yellow with five petals, and they typically grow at the ends of branches, and can be clustered.
